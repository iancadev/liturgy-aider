When you reduce every component of the Liturgy Aid to very basic layouts, it actually seems very simple.

(Except for all the space-filling clip-art. But that's out of scope.)

### Categorization 1 - groupings

Letterhead / license footer

Label signifier (for an event during the Mass)

Label + text

Label + music

> note: Sanctus also has an additional music sub-label (e.g. "New Rite" or "A Community Mass")
>
> Kyrie also has an additional sub-label ("Cantor intones, then all response")

### Categorization 2 - types of labels

Boxed label

Underline label (w/ subtitle)

Bold label (w/ subtitle)

Free-floating label (w/ subtitle)

### Categorization 3 - types of layouts

Label middle and above, music/text centered

Label left and above, music/text centered

Label to the left, music/text to the right

Label to the left, music/text centered

Label to the left, music/text next to on the right (shifted as a group)


Subtitle below label

Subtitle to the right of the label


(Rare: 6PM opening chant) Label above, text below + left, music below + right

(For communion) Label above, music centered, verses centered below

### Categorization 4 - types of space-filling schemes

Put text in the center -- tall, narrow span

Squeeze into one horizontal line

Make the text larger + more vertical spacing

Make the hymn full-width

Keep everything together, and move into the middle or to the left

Make the center text smaller or larger (it's unimportant / free-floating)

### Categorization 5 - types of components (based on liturgical function)

Textmatter

Prayers

Readings

Waypoints

Events (with information)

Hymns/chants (with translations)

Sung responses



> This isn't perfect. Penitential Rite and May the Lord are prayers with a responsorial feel.
>
> "Our Father" and "Prayer to Saint Michael the Archangel" have a supplicating feel.
